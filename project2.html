Flex.



Role :

UI/UX. Visual Design.

started :

Nov 2017.

completed :

Dec 2017.

Flex is an all-in-one digital wallet solution that combines mobile pay and mobile identification storage in one platform. Flex uses RFID technology to transfer information efficiently and securely, all from your smartphone or smartwatch. The project was conceptualized and executed over the course of the Interaction Design 1 semester and marked my first forray into a large design project.

The creative process started with brainstorming and competetive analysis. This was followed by user studies, interviews, and storyboards to identify painpoints within user flows. Next was site mapping and low fidelity wire framing for mobile and desktop. After copious user testing and UI revision, I moved on to branding and visual design. Finally, I created high fidelity interactive mockups that went through several phases of user testing and feedback. From concept to creation, Project Flex provided firsthand experience with varying stages of development in an entrepreneurial setting.










Piece of Cake.



Role :

UI/UX, Design

started :

Feb 2018

completed :

April 2018

Piece of Cake is an app designed to help groups make decisions on where to eat for dinner by utilizing gamification. Each of the players is assigned a role which determines how they play the game and reach a decision! Piece of Cake was designed to integrate with the Yelp API and for the scope of the project, designed for five or more players. The project was conceptualized and executed over the course of the Interaction Design 2 semester and built off the foundations of the Interaction Design 1 curriculum. Due to the complex nature of the app, a large part of my focus was on gamification research and developing a game-like infrastructure.

Much like project Flex, the creative process started with brainstorming, research, and competetive analysis. My early research lead me to the consideration of game theory and alternate voting systems (runoff, first-pass, ranked, and preferential). This was followed by user studies, interviews, and storyboards to identify painpoints. Next was site mapping, user flows, low fidelity wire framing for mobile, and paper prototyping.


User flows proved to be on of the most useful design tools during this project, as the complexity continued to skyrocket with each new feature or gameplay model. Paper prototyping also proved to be a useful tool, allowing me to complete insightful user tests with my contemporaries, and taking the product outside of the digital world into the physical world. After copious user testing and UI revision, I moved on to branding and visual design.


Finally, I created high fidelity interactive mockups that went through several phases of user testing and feedback. Thanks to a more generous timeline for the project, I was afforded extra time in the early prototyping phase, allowing me to take part in more rigorous user testing. From concept to creation, Project Piece of Cake built off my prior experience with gaming and interaction design to bring the product to life!
